Risk Management Glossary

Comprehensive definitions of key financial risk management terms

Market Risk Terms

Backtesting

The process of comparing actual returns with expected returns to validate the accuracy of risk models, particularly VaR models. Regulators typically require banks to backtest their VaR models using at least one year of historical data.

Market Risk Learn more

Delta

A risk measure that estimates the change in the price of a derivative, such as an option, relative to the change in the price of the underlying asset. Delta is the first derivative of the option's value with respect to the underlying asset's price.

Market Risk Learn more

Historical Simulation

A method for calculating Value at Risk (VaR) that uses historical data to create a distribution of potential portfolio returns. It applies historical changes in market variables to the current portfolio to simulate potential future returns.

Market Risk Learn more

Monte Carlo Simulation

A computational technique that uses repeated random sampling to obtain numerical results for risk assessment. In market risk, it's used to simulate various market scenarios and their impact on portfolio value.

Market Risk Learn more

Stress Testing

A simulation technique used to determine the ability of a financial institution to deal with an economic crisis. It involves testing the resilience of institutions to various scenarios, including extreme market movements.

Market Risk Learn more

Value at Risk (VaR)

A statistical technique used to measure and quantify the level of financial risk within a firm or investment portfolio over a specific time frame. VaR is defined as the maximum potential loss in value of a portfolio due to adverse market movements, for a given confidence level, over a defined period.

Market Risk Learn more

Credit Risk Terms

Credit Default Swap (CDS)

A financial derivative that allows an investor to swap or offset their credit risk with that of another investor. It's essentially an insurance against default, where the buyer makes payments to the seller until the contract matures, and in return, receives a payoff if the underlying financial instrument defaults.

Credit Risk Learn more

Expected Loss (EL)

The average credit loss that a lender expects to incur in a given time period. It's calculated as the product of Probability of Default (PD), Loss Given Default (LGD), and Exposure at Default (EAD): EL = PD × LGD × EAD.

Credit Risk Learn more

Loss Given Default (LGD)

The amount of money a bank or other financial institution loses when a borrower defaults on a loan, expressed as a percentage of total exposure at the time of default. LGD = 1 - Recovery Rate.

Credit Risk Learn more

Probability of Default (PD)

The likelihood that a borrower will default on their debt obligations over a given time horizon, typically one year. PD is a key component in calculating expected loss and regulatory capital requirements.

Credit Risk Learn more

Counterparty Risk Terms

Credit Valuation Adjustment (CVA)

An adjustment to the valuation of a derivative transaction to account for the credit risk of the counterparty. CVA represents the market value of counterparty credit risk and is calculated as the difference between the risk-free portfolio value and the true portfolio value that takes into account the possibility of a counterparty's default.

Counterparty Risk Learn more

Debt Valuation Adjustment (DVA)

The counterparty risk adjustment from the perspective of the institution itself. DVA represents the market value of the institution's own credit risk to its counterparties, and is essentially the CVA from the counterparty's perspective.

Counterparty Risk Learn more

Potential Future Exposure (PFE)

A measure of counterparty risk that estimates the maximum expected credit exposure over a specified period of time at a specified confidence level. PFE is calculated using simulation techniques to model potential future market scenarios.

Counterparty Risk Learn more

Wrong-Way Risk

The risk that occurs when exposure to a counterparty is adversely correlated with the credit quality of that counterparty. In other words, as the creditworthiness of the counterparty deteriorates, the exposure to that counterparty increases.

Counterparty Risk Learn more

Regulatory Terms

Basel III

A global regulatory framework for more resilient banks and banking systems, developed by the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision. It builds on Basel II and introduces new capital and liquidity requirements to strengthen the regulation, supervision, and risk management of the banking sector.

Regulatory Learn more

FRTB (Fundamental Review of the Trading Book)

A set of rules developed by the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision that aims to overhaul how banks calculate market risk capital requirements. FRTB introduces a revised standardized approach and internal models approach for market risk.

Regulatory Learn more

Internal Ratings-Based (IRB) Approach

A method allowed under Basel II and III that enables banks to use their internal models to estimate credit risk parameters (PD, LGD, EAD) for regulatory capital calculations. The IRB approach can be either Foundation IRB (F-IRB) or Advanced IRB (A-IRB).

Regulatory Learn more

General Risk Management Terms

Diversification

A risk management technique that mixes a wide variety of investments within a portfolio to minimize the impact of any single investment's performance on the overall portfolio performance. The rationale is that a portfolio constructed of different kinds of assets will, on average, yield higher long-term returns and lower the risk of any individual holding or security.

General

Hedging

A risk management strategy used to offset potential losses in investments by taking an opposite position in a related asset. The goal of hedging is not to make money but to protect from losses.

General

Risk Appetite

The level of risk that an organization is willing to accept in pursuit of its objectives. Risk appetite is a key component of an organization's risk management framework and helps guide decision-making processes.
